<section class="introduction" id="introduction-3"><p id="p-201">The chromatic number of a graph is defined as the least amount of colours necessary to colour the vertices of a graph in such a way that no two adjacent vertices have the same colour. In this chapter we investigate upper and lower bounds on the chromatic number, after which we consider planar graphs. We conclude with the famous 4-colour theorem.</p></section><nav class="summary-links"><ul>
