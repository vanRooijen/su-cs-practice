<div id="content" class="pretext-content"><section class="preface" id="preface"><h2 class="heading hide-type">
<span class="type">Preface</span> <span class="codenumber"></span> <span class="title">Preface</span>
</h2>
<p id="p-1">This text is intended for the undergraduate course Applied Discrete Mathematics 314 as presented at Stellenbosch University. The text is divided into three parts: an introduction to graph theory; cryptography and coding theory.</p>
<p id="p-2">The first part gives a short introduction to the basic notation and terminology of graph theory, afterwhich topics like planarity and graph colourings are considered. This part of the notes was originally compiled by Dr. P.J.P. Grobler from Stellenboschh University.</p>
<p id="p-3">The second part provides an introduction to cryptography with a focus on public key cryptography and the mathematics behind it. This part of the notes is loosely based on the <em class="emphasis">Handbook of Applied Cryptology</em>, by A. Menezes, P. van Oorschot, and S. Vanstone, and is available online at <a class="external" href="http://www.cacr.math.uwaterloo.ca/hac" target="_blank"><code class="code-inline tex2jax_ignore">http://www.cacr.math.uwaterloo.ca/hac</code></a>.</p>
<p id="p-4">The third and final part serves as an introduction to coding theory. The focus falls mainly on error-correcting codes and we take a closer look at linear and cyclic codes. This part of the notes is loosely based on the <em class="emphasis">A first course in coding theory</em>, by R. Hill.</p></section></div>
