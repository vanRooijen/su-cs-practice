<div class="newsitem"><h1>International Conferences at Stellenbosch</h1><div class="newsdate"><span class="day"> Sunday </span><span class="date"> 14 </span><span class="month">October</span><span class="year">2018</span></div><p>The Division of Computer Science is organizing two international conferences at Stias, the 14th African Conference on Research in Computer Science, <a href="http://www.cari-info.org/">CARI</a>, and the 15th International Colloquium on Theoretical Aspects of Computing, <a href="https://www.ictac.org.za/">ICTAC</a>.</p><p>CARI (<a href="http://www.cari-info.org/">http://www.cari-info.org/</a>), the African Conference on Research in Computer Science, is the outcome of an international cooperation involving African universities, French research institutes, and international organizations that takes place every other year. It brings together African researchers, and decision-makers in the fields of computer science and applied mathematics. This year marks the 14th time the event is held, but the first time it takes place outside the francophone area in Africa. Its program comprises 28 research presentations covering diverse topics in computer science and applied mathematics.</p><p>The International Colloquium on Theoretical Aspects of Computing (ICTAC, <a href="https://www.ictac.org.za/">https://www.ictac.org.za/</a>) was established in 2004 by the International Institute for Software Technology of the United Nations University (UNU-IIST). It aims at bringing together researchers and practitioners from academia, industry and government to present research and exchange ideas and experience addressing challenges in both theoretical aspects of computing and the exploitation of theory through methods and tools for system development. The program of ICTAC 2018 consists of 4 invited talks and 25 contributed papers, which were selected from 59 submissions by an international program committee chaired by Prof Tarmo Uustalu (<a href="http://www.ru.is/faculty/tarmo">http://www.ru.is/faculty/tarmo</a>), Reykjavik University.</p><p>Both events are complemented by a tutorial program that includes seven half-day tutorials, and two workshops. The entire event was supported by Stellenbosch University, IFIP TC6 and DEC, Inria, AUF, Cirad, and IRD.</p></div>
