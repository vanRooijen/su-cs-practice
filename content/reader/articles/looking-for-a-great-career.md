## What about the future?

Digital systems now sit at the center of nearly every sector: science, finance, health, education, logistics, and public services. A Computer Science background gives you a broad technical foundation that can branch into many specialist roles over time.

## Career paths to explore

- **Software Developer**: design, build, and maintain production software systems.
  [Role profile](https://www.bls.gov/ooh/computer-and-information-technology/software-developers.htm)
- **Information Security Analyst**: protect systems, services, and data against attack.
  [Role profile](https://www.bls.gov/ooh/computer-and-information-technology/information-security-analysts.htm)
- **Data and AI Engineer / Scientist**: build data pipelines and machine-learning driven products.
  [Computer and Information Research Scientists](https://www.bls.gov/ooh/computer-and-information-technology/computer-and-information-research-scientists.htm)
- **Database and Data Platform Specialist**: model, operate, and optimize critical data infrastructure.
  [Role profile](https://www.bls.gov/ooh/computer-and-information-technology/database-administrators.htm)
- **Web and Application Engineer**: deliver modern web platforms and user-facing digital products.
  [Role profile](https://www.bls.gov/ooh/computer-and-information-technology/web-developers.htm)
- **Systems Analyst**: align technical solutions with organizational goals and workflows.
  [Role profile](https://www.bls.gov/ooh/computer-and-information-technology/computer-systems-analysts.htm)
