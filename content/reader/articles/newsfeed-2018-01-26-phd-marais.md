<div class="newsitem"><h1>PhD Defense: Laurette Marais</h1><div class="newsdate"><span class="day"> Friday </span><span class="date"> 26 </span><span class="month">January</span><span class="year">2018</span></div><p class="noimage floatright"><img src="/cs-assets/newsfeed/phd-marais.jpg" alt="Laurette Marais"></p><p class="nodisplay">Laurette Marais will defend her PhD entitled “Generalised Acceptance Conditions for Symmetric Difference Nondeterministic Finite Automata” on Friday 26 January 2018, 15:00-16:00 in Room A521.</p><h5 id="candidate">Candidate</h5><p>Laurette Marais</p><h5 id="title">Title</h5><p>Generalised Acceptance Conditions for Symmetric Difference Nondeterministic Finite Automata</p><h5 id="date">Date</h5><p>Friday 26 January 2018, 15:00-16:00</p><h5 id="venue">Venue</h5><p>Room A521, General Engineering Building</p><h5 id="supervisor">Supervisor</h5><p>Prof Lynette van Zijl</p><h5 id="abstract">Abstract</h5><p>Symmetric difference nondeterministic finite state automata (XNFA) are an instance of generalised nondeterminism, of which the behaviour is represented by the sym- metric difference of all possible computation paths. We introduce the notion of <em>generalised acceptance</em> for XNFA, and investigate descriptional complexity issues related to two specific instances, namely self-verifying XNFA (SV-XNFA) and *-XNFA.</p><p>For SV-XNFA, we apply self-verifying acceptance, originally defined for typical nondeterministic finite state automata (NFA), to XNFA. Self-verification involves defining a set of accept states, as well as a set of reject states, and requires that the automaton give an explicit accept or reject result on any input. We provide state complexity bounds for determinising unary and non-unary SV-XNFA.</p><p>We define *-XNFA as XNFA with any finite number of final sets, while * rep- resents a left-associative set operation on the language associated with each set of final states. We investigate and compare the descriptional complexity of various lan- guage operations, namely intersection, union, relative complement (or difference), symmetric difference and complement, for unary XNFA and unary *-XNFA.</p><p><em><strong>All PhD defences are open to the public and you are welcome to attend. Please note that seating may be limited.</strong></em></p></div>
